Extension Methods Used

K-Tip hair extensions, also known as keratin bonds, are a popular method for adding length and volume. This technique involves attaching individual strands to natural hair using a keratin-based adhesive, which is melted with a specialized heating tool for a durable and seamless blend.

The Application Process

A meticulous strand-by-strand application is the hallmark of K-Tip extensions. A trained stylist sections the hair and uses a heating tool to soften the keratin tip, carefully molding it around a small section of natural hair to create a strong, sealed bond.

Longevity and Maintenance

With proper care, K-Tips can last from three to five months. As the natural hair grows, the bonds move down the hair shaft. Regular maintenance is not required for tightening, as the bonds are not reusable. Instead, the full set is removed and a new one is applied.

I-Tip, or Nano Bead, extensions are a discreet method using individual strands attached with a tiny bead or nano ring. This is a "cold fusion" technique, meaning no heat or glue is required, making it a gentler option.

The Application Process

A stylist sections the hair, threads a small, silicone-lined bead onto a strand of natural hair, inserts the I-Tip extension into the bead, and then clamps the bead firmly with pliers to secure it.

Maintenance

Every 6-8 weeks, a "move-up" appointment is necessary. The stylist will open the beads, slide the extensions back up to the scalp, and re-clamp them, allowing the hair to be reused multiple times.

Tape-in extensions are a popular semi-permanent solution that involves sandwiching a thin section of natural hair between two wefts with medical-grade adhesive. They are known for their quick application and natural-looking results.

The Application Process

On clean, dry hair, a stylist takes a thin section of natural hair and applies one tape-in weft underneath and another on top, pressing them together to create a secure "sandwich" bond.

Removal and Re-application

After 6-8 weeks, a professional uses a special alcohol-based solution to break down the adhesive, allowing the wefts to be gently removed. The hair is often reusable after the old tape is cleaned off and new adhesive is applied.

Hand-tied wefts are a premium method known for their natural appearance. Unlike machine-made wefts, they are handcrafted to be thinner and lighter, allowing them to lay flat against the scalp for a seamless blend.

The Application Process

Using a "beaded row" technique, a stylist creates a foundation by attaching a row of small, silicone-lined beads to the natural hair. The hand-tied wefts are then carefully sewn onto this beaded track, distributing weight evenly and minimizing tension.

Maintenance

With proper care, the hair can be reused for up to a year. Move-up appointments are required every 6-8 weeks, where the stylist removes the wefts and beads and reinstalls them closer to the scalp.
